## 3. Information We Collect

### 3.1 Information You Provide to Us

**(a) Account Registration Information**
When you create an account, we collect your name, email address, date of birth (for age verification), and password. Merchants additionally provide business name, business address, tax identification number, and other information required for KYC compliance and Stripe Connect onboarding.

**(b) Transaction Information**
When you make a purchase, we collect transaction details including the deal purchased, purchase amount, and transaction date. Payment card data is processed directly by Stripe and is not stored by Crunchy Plum. We receive limited payment confirmation data from Stripe (such as the last four digits of your card and card type) for transaction records.

**(c) Communications**
When you contact our customer support team, submit a refund request, or communicate with us through the Platform, we collect the content of those communications and your contact information.

**(d) Merchant-Submitted Content**
Merchants provide Deal descriptions, pricing information, business images, and other content submitted through the merchant dashboard.

### 3.2 Information We Collect Automatically

**(a) Device and Usage Information**
When you access the Platform, we automatically collect information about your device and how you interact with our services, including:
- Device type, operating system, and browser type
- IP address
- Pages viewed, features used, and time spent on the Platform
- Referring URLs and exit pages
- Crash reports and performance data

**(b) Location Data**
With your permission, we collect your precise geographic location (GPS data) through the mobile application. We use location data to show you deals near your current location and to improve the relevance of deal recommendations. You may disable location access at any time through your device settings. Disabling location access will not prevent you from using the Platform, but may limit the accuracy of location-based deal recommendations.

We do not share your precise GPS location data with Merchants or any third party, except as required by law.

**(c) Cookies and Tracking Technologies**
We use cookies, pixel tags, and similar technologies to operate the Platform and collect usage data. See Section 7 for more details.

### 3.3 Information from Third Parties

**(a) Stripe**
We receive payment confirmation and fraud signal data from Stripe in connection with transaction processing.

**(b) Google Analytics**
We use Google Analytics to collect aggregated data about how users interact with the Platform, including page views, session duration, and navigation patterns. Google Analytics may set cookies on your device. For more information about how Google uses data collected through Analytics, visit: policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.

## 7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

### 7.1 Types of Cookies We Use

| Cookie Type | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Essential cookies | Required for Platform functionality, including login sessions and shopping cart |
| Analytics cookies | Google Analytics — collecting aggregated usage data to improve the Platform |
| Preference cookies | Remembering your settings and preferences |

### 7.2 Google Analytics
We use Google Analytics to understand how users interact with our Platform. Google Analytics collects data such as pages visited, time spent, device type, and general geographic location (derived from IP address, not GPS). This data is aggregated and does not identify you personally to us. ### 7.3 Managing Cookies
You may control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling essential cookies may affect the functionality of the Platform. Disabling analytics cookies will not affect your ability to use the Platform.

### 7.4 Do Not Track
Some browsers transmit "Do Not Track" signals to websites. We do not currently respond to Do Not Track signals because no uniform industry standard for recognizing or honoring such signals has been established. We will update this Policy if our practices change or if an applicable standard is adopted.

## 9. Your Privacy Rights

### 9.1 Rights Under Texas Law (TDPSA)
If you are a Texas resident, you have the following rights with respect to your personal information:

**(a) Right to Know / Access:** You have the right to confirm whether we are processing your personal information and to request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.

**(b) Right to Correction:** You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal information we hold about you.

**(c) Right to Deletion:** You have the right to request deletion of personal information we have collected from or about you, subject to certain exceptions (such as information we are required to retain by law).

**(d) Right to Data Portability:** You have the right to obtain a copy of your personal information in a portable and, to the extent technically feasible, readily usable format.

**(e) Right to Opt Out of Targeted Advertising:** You have the right to opt out of the processing of your personal information for purposes of targeted advertising. Crunchy Plum does not currently engage in targeted advertising using your personal data.

**(f) Right to Opt Out of Sale:** Crunchy Plum does not sell personal information. No opt-out mechanism for sale is required, but you may contact us to confirm our practices.

**(g) Right to Non-Discrimination:** We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your privacy rights.

### 9.2 Rights Under California Law (CCPA/CPRA)
If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including the right to know about personal information collected, disclosed, or sold; the right to delete personal information; the right to correct inaccurate personal information; the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information; and the right to limit use of sensitive personal information. ### 12.3 Data Breach Notification

Notification will be provided by email to your registered email address and, where required by law, to the Texas Attorney General's office.
